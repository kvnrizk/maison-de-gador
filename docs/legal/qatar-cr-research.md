# Qatar Commercial Registration — Research

## Summary

E-commerce food businesses in Qatar require:
1. Commercial Registration (CR) from Ministry of Commerce
2. Trade License from Baladiya (requires physical premises)
3. MOPH Food Establishment Registration (Wathiq system)
4. Food Handlers Certificates for all staff
5. Tax Identification Number (TIN)

## Key Costs (QAR)

| Name registration | 2,000 (AR) / 4,000 (EN) |

## Important Notes

- 100% foreign ownership allowed (Law No. 1 of 2019)
- Physical premises required even for e-commerce (kitchen/warehouse)
- HACCP certificate needed for MOPH registration
- GCC 2025-2026: biodegradable packaging required (PFAS-free)
- Timeline: 1-8 weeks for trade license alone

## Process Steps

### 1. Reserve Trade Name
- Apply via Ministry of Commerce portal (moci.gov.qa)
- Name in Arabic required; English optional (additional fee)
- Processing: 1-3 business days

### 2. Draft Articles of Association
- For LLC: minimum 2 shareholders
- For sole proprietorship: simpler process
- Notarization required

### 3. Register with Ministry of Commerce
- Submit CR application with trade name approval
- Attach Articles of Association
- Pay registration fees
- Receive CR certificate

### 4. Chamber of Commerce Registration
- Mandatory after CR
- Annual membership fee
- Required for import/export licenses

### 5. Municipality Trade License (Baladiya)
- Requires physical premises inspection
- Food safety requirements must be met
- Annual renewal

### 6. MOPH Food Registration (Wathiq)
- Register food establishment on Wathiq system
- HACCP certification required
- All food handlers must have valid health cards
- Regular inspections after registration

## Food-Specific Requirements

- **HACCP Certification**: Required for all food businesses. Can get through accredited bodies in Qatar.
- **Food Handler Health Cards**: Each worker needs a health card from MOPH (medical examination).
- **Labeling**: All products must have Arabic labels with ingredients, allergens, expiry date, storage conditions.
- **Packaging**: Must comply with GCC standards (GSO). Biodegradable packaging increasingly required.
- **Import**: If importing ingredients (chocolate, nuts), need import license and food safety clearance.

## E-Commerce Specific

- E-commerce regulation: Law No. 16 of 2010 on e-transactions
- Must display: business name, CR number, contact details on website
- Clear return/refund policy required
- Payment processor must be QCB-licensed (Dibsy is QCB compliant)
- Data protection: follow Qatar's Data Privacy Law (Law No. 13 of 2016)
