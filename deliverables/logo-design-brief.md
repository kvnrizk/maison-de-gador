## Brand Overview

- **Brand Name**: La Maison de Gador (with accent: GADôR)
- **Tagline (English)**: "The Art of Adoration"
- **Tagline (Arabic)**: "فن التذوق" (The Art of Tasting)
- **Industry**: Premium/luxury stuffed dates and chocolate dates
- **Market**: Qatar (Gulf region)
- **Competitors**: Bateel, Qinwan, Forrey & Galland

---

## The Symbol: Phoenix

The brand uses a **phoenix** as its symbol. This is unique in the dates market — no competitor uses it.

**Keep the phoenix. It's a strong differentiator.**

### Phoenix Direction:
- **Style**: Elegant, refined, geometric — NOT overly illustrated or cartoon-like
- **Pose**: Wings spread upward (like the current golden version)
- **Details**: Simplified feather lines, clean silhouette. Should be recognizable at 32x32px (favicon size)
- **Accents**: Small teal/turquoise flame details on the crest and tail tips

### What NOT to do:
- No hyper-detailed illustration (the old teal phoenix is too complex)
- No thick outlines
- No 3D effects or gradients in the primary version

## Typography Direction

### Brand Name: "La Maison de Gador"
- **Style**: Elegant serif font with slight contrast (thin/thick strokes)
- **Reference fonts**: Playfair Display, Cormorant Garamond, Didot, Bodoni
- **Case**: Title case — "La Maison de GADôR" (keep the accent on the ô)
- **Weight**: Medium to semi-bold

### Tagline: "The Art of Adoration"
- **Style**: Light sans-serif or italicized serif
- **Size**: Smaller, understated below the brand name

### Arabic Text
- **Style**: Modern Naskh or Kufi-inspired
- **Reference fonts**: Cairo, Tajawal, or a custom Arabic font that matches the English serif
- **Usage**: Arabic version of the logo for packaging and bilingual materials
